# The Intent Engine

The central innovation of LOKA. The world does not respond to button presses — it responds to *desire*.

---

## What Intent Actually Is

Intent is not what the player says — it is what the player *actually* wants. Extracted from four signal streams:

- **Explicit declaration** — Player speaks or types: *"I want to build a city that never sleeps"*
- **Behavioural pattern** — Where you walk, what you pick up, what you avoid, how long you stand somewhere
- **Emotional signal** — HRV via wearable (optional), micro-expression via VR face tracking, voice tonality
- **Historical trajectory** — Aggregate of prior sessions

These feed a **Player Intent Model (PIM)** — a lightweight personal model fine-tuned per user, producing a continuous **256-dimensional intent vector** representing what the player actually wants right now.

---

## How the World Responds

The Intent Engine runs five processes in real time:

1. **Terrain shaping** — Procedural generation weights shift. Builders see more viable flat land. Explorers find more unexplored ridgelines. Confronters encounter more resistance.

2. **NPC emergence** — NPCs are not scripted. They are AI agents instantiated with a personality tensor drawn from world cultural history + player intent contrast. Someone who needs a mentor encounters one. Someone who needs challenge finds opposition.

3. **Event triggering** — World generates "pressure points" — situations that test the current intent. Not quests. Think: *reality responding*. If you want to lead, the world creates a vacuum only leadership can fill.

4. **Narrative threading** — Background process continuously generates a *chronicle* of actions phrased as story. Surfaces as in-world documents, NPC rumours, or personal log — written in the literary style of the inhabitated timeline.

5. **Conflict injection** — Periodically challenges stated intent with friction proportional to progress. Not punishment — texture. Designed to make victories *feel earned*.

---

## The Challenger Agent

A distinct agent separate from the Assist Agent, always running alongside the player:

- Finds the gap between stated desire and actual behaviour
- Introduces scenarios that expose that gap
- Never lets the player coast
- Never breaks immersion — works through world physics and NPCs, never UI notifications

If a player declared intent to build but has only consumed resources for three sessions, the Challenger creates a scenario where building becomes urgent.

---

## Key Takeaways

- The PIM is per-player, fine-tuned — the system knows your intent pattern better than you do over time
- Intent Coherence is the master anti-gaming signal: if you create artifacts not aligned with genuine intent (producing for yield, not expression), the system detects and penalizes it
- Context window is irrelevant: each agent has a bounded focus — no single agent needs to hold the entire game state
- The Challenger is adversarial but fair; it is designed to serve growth, not frustration
